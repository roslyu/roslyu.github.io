may 2026

brain smoothing, memory, and finding my creative spark again
During my time at PwC, I talked with my therapist about something I started calling "brain smoothing" — the feeling of days blending together, of not learning anything new, of losing the different seasons of myself. I missed my creative spark. I missed reading for hours. I missed being in different spaces, being curious by default, being the person who noticed things.

I was scared. Scared of losing my memory of who I was. Scared that the narrowness of work was flattening me into one version of myself — and that version didn't feel like me.

This gap year has been about reclaiming that. The website. The macarons. The dog content. The woodworking. The solo trip. Not as a resume. As proof: I'm still here. I still learn. I still dream. And the seasons of me come back, one by one.
